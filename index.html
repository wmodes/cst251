Wes Modes is a California artist focused on social practice, sculpture, performance and new media work. Their pioneering social practice work with the people who live in American river communities has been nationally recognized and reported worldwide. Their multidisciplinary work Secret History of American River People combines social practice, oral history, video, photography, sculpture, new media, and adventurous fieldwork on American rivers since 2014. (peoplesriverhistory.us)

Wes' sculpture, photography, and new media works have appeared in group and solo shows since 1996. Their performance and social practice work since the mid-1990 has made headlines internationally and has been chronicled in journals. Their comic work has been published in several noted illustrated histories. (modes.io)

Wes holds a Masters in Fine Arts from the Digital Art and New Media program at the University of California Santa Cruz. Wes is a lecturer at the University of California Santa Cruz and California State University Monterey Bay since 2015. They served as the summer curator and exhibition catalyst at the Santa Cruz Museum of Art & History in 2016.

Wes worked in the tech industry for 25 years as a software engineer, systems administrator, and systems architect. They are also a writer, performer, adventurer and community organizer.
